# IPAS Overview
This document was created to provide an overview of the project.

## What is IPAS?
IPAS is an intelligent air pollution analysis and monitoring system. The data will be stored in a _rawdata.json_ file, which will later be read by the website. We do not display the data directly in _HTML_ because that would significantly reduce its potential for further processing.

We aim to progress step by step. The current goal for _V1_ is to read sensor data and display it on the website. Later, we plan to implement trend analysis and eventually integrate artificial intelligence for predictions. In the future, we also plan to develop a dedicated mobile application.

## Why was IPAS created?
IPAS started as a high school project, aimed at introducing project-based learning for students in grades 7–8. The goal was to create a project that could also serve as a strong addition to a university portfolio, while introducing younger students to modern research methods and STREAM education.

The project has also received support from the Hungarian government through a grant.

## Where can IPAS be used?
The prototype is currently under development, but the goal is for it to be usable in almost any environment. It will be equipped with a solar panel and an internal battery, with the aim of operating independently for up to 24 hours.

The outer enclosure is planned to be 3D printed and weather-resistant. The current _V1_ version will not include a SIM card slot, so it will connect to a local Wi-Fi network and the website will only be accessible within that network. This limitation is planned to be addressed and improved in _V2_.
